Citizen journalism, like many aspects of life, has its pros and cons.

On the positive side, citizen journalism has allowed the public a platform where they can fact check the news given out to them by traditional news outlets. This provides multiple perspectives towards a single topic, which can lead to constructive discussions among the public. Citizen journalism also gives smaller communities a voice and a platform accessible to the entire world. This accessibility to foreign information connects the world and raises awareness of issues in different communities. And most importantly, citizen journalism allows for the rapid spread of information regarding crises. A good example of this in practice is the Boston Marathon Bombings in 2013: a soon as news broke out, citizen journalists took to social media to search for the culprits of the crime.

With everything good there is always the bad, and citizen journalism is not without its faults. For one, citizen journalists may generate their reports with a bias towards a certain perspective. This and a lack of training in fact checking can lead to the spread of false information to the rest of the world. A domino affect can be seen in situations like this, where the false news spread by citizens can be used by professionals, ultimately spreading the incorrect news to an even larger audience.

The rise of citizen journalism along side social media has certainly caused both positive and negative situations. Now more than ever, it is imperative that people keep a level of skepticism when reading the news in order to spot and prevent the spread of false information.
