Designs created in DES 16: Design Graphics and DES 115: Letterforms and Type

626 Night Market Poster

This piece was part of a class project where we were tasked with creating a poster of an event of our choosing. One of the difficulites that came with creating this design was maintaining the stylistic choices of the event, while also incorporating your own style. To solve this problem, I looked at different posters that the Night Market had created for past events to get an idea of what their color schemes, font, and image choices were. From there, I did many iterations of my poster using both Adobe Illustrator and Adobe Photoshop, until I finally decided on the design that best represented the event.

Georgia O'Keeffe Poster

The Georgia O'Keeffe poster was another class project, with the assignment being to create a poster that celebrates an influential person in the arts or sciences. I chose Georgia O'Keeffe because I have great respect for much of her paintings. For the poster design, I decided to exemplify her large art pieces by scaling them to be about (or larger) than the photo bust. Additionally, because one of her art mediums was watercolor, I used a special brush to give the edge of the paintings a fading effect.

Paul Rand Book Design

This book design project was a full re-design of Paul Rand's "Design and the Play Instinct." We were all given the text within the book, as well as the photos that accompanied them. A big issue that I ran into while creating this design was the orientation of the text in relation to the photos. I found it hard to create page spreads that effectively showcased the images, while not overshadowing the text content. However, I eventually found an effective way of representing all the required data after multiple iterations creatied in Adobe InDesign.

Pen & Paper Designs

Projects created in DES 14: Design Drawing

Graphite Cup

For this project, we were tasked with sketching and shading an enlarged photo of an object of our choice. We needed to use all the sketching techniques learned in class: stippling, cross-hatching, etc. A major hurdle for me when creating this project was using the right technique to effectively show the details of the cup. This took mutliple re-dos due to the difficulty of this project.

Still-Life Ink and Watercolor

Using both pen and watercolor, we were tasked with illustrating a still life piece. The piece we created on our own, and it needed to have a woven basket, rocks, a blanket, a round object, leaves, and a feather. One of the difficult parts of this project was collecting the materials and placing them in a stylistic way. I had to do multiple different orientation of the objects before finally deciding on one.

Pen Skeleton

For this project, we were given a print out of a skeleton and were tasked with replecating it on a larger scale. A major issue when drawing this was getting the size and proportions of the skeleton correct. The hands ended up being a little bit to big in the final design, but I'm proud of how the details came out in the end.
